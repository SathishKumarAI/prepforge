question: "What is the difference between Breadth-First Search (BFS) and Depth-First Search (DFS)?"

Imagine exploring a huge underground maze. With **Breadth-First Search (BFS)**, you explore cautiously in rings: check every room one step away, then every room two steps away, and so on, like ripples spreading on a pond. Because you sweep each distance fully before going farther, you're guaranteed to find the nearest exit first, but you must remember a *lot* of rooms at once. With **Depth-First Search (DFS)**, you instead pick one corridor and charge straight down it until you hit a dead end, then back up and try the next. You barely have to remember anything, just your current path, but you might sprint down a long wrong tunnel and miss a closer exit. Rule of thumb: use BFS when the answer is probably nearby or you need the shortest route; use DFS when memory is tight or answers lie deep.
