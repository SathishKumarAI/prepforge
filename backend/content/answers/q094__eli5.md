question: "What are Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCDs) and what are the common types?"
topic: "Data Analytics"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []

A "dimension" in a warehouse is descriptive info, like a customer's details. A **slowly changing dimension** is about what you do when that info changes, say a customer moves to a new city. Do you keep the old city or not?

Think of updating an address book. **Type 1** is erasing the old address and writing the new one, simple, but the past is gone forever. **Type 2** is adding a brand-new entry for the new address while keeping the old one, each stamped with dates and a "current" flag. Now you have the full history and can see where they lived at any past moment, this is the most common choice. **Type 3** keeps just a "previous address" column, remembering only the last change. (Type 0 means it never changes at all.)

Why it matters: Type 2 lets you report data "as it truly was" back then, essential for accurate history; Type 1 trades that history for simplicity.
