question: "What is the Turing Test and what does it measure?"
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"

Imagine a texting game where you chat with two hidden strangers at once. One is a real person, the other is a machine, and you can't see or hear either, only read their messages. Your job is to guess which is which. That's the heart of the **Turing Test**, proposed by Alan Turing in 1950. If, after chatting, you honestly can't tell the machine from the human, the machine is said to pass. Notice what this actually measures: whether the machine *acts* human in conversation, not whether it truly understands or feels anything. A machine could give all the right replies while grasping nothing, the way you might ace a quiz by memorizing answers. That's why many argue passing the test proves good imitation, not real thinking. Today's chatbots often fool people, which reopens the whole debate.
