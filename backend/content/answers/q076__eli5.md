Think of the four types of analytics like a visit to the doctor, each question harder and more valuable than the last.

**Descriptive** asks *what happened?* — it's the thermometer reading and your chart: last month's sales, this quarter's signups. Just the facts.

**Diagnostic** asks *why did it happen?* — the doctor digging into causes: sales dropped *because* a competitor cut prices.

**Predictive** asks *what's likely next?* — using patterns to forecast, like warning "at this rate you'll get sick" (or "customers like these tend to cancel").

**Prescriptive** asks *what should we do about it?* — the actual prescription: the recommended action, like adjusting prices or a treatment plan.

As you climb from describing to prescribing, the work gets more complex but the payoff grows. Most companies start by simply describing the past and gradually mature toward predicting and prescribing — and each stage needs a solid one beneath it.
