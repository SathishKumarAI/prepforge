question: "What is funnel analysis and how do you use it?"

A funnel is a set of steps people take toward a goal, in order, like visited the store, added to cart, started checkout, paid. It's called a funnel because fewer people remain at each step, the way a real funnel narrows.

**Funnel analysis** counts how many people make it from one step to the next, and, more importantly, where they fall out. At each stage you calculate a conversion rate: people who reached this step divided by people at the step before.

The magic is spotting the biggest drop-off. If 90% add to cart but only 20% start checkout, that huge leak is where fixing things pays off most. You then slice the funnel by device or channel to see who's struggling, form a guess about why, and test a fix (often an A/B test). It turns "sales are low" into "here's exactly where we lose people."
