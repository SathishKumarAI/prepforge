question: "What is the difference between symbolic AI and connectionist (neural) AI?"
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Picture two very different cooks. The first follows a **rulebook**: explicit if-this-then-that instructions written by an expert — "if the sauce is too thin, add flour." That's **symbolic AI**: knowledge stored as clear symbols, logic, and rules. It's easy to read and great at step-by-step reasoning, but brittle — give it a messy, unexpected situation and it stalls, because someone had to write down every rule. The second cook never opened a book; they learned by **tasting thousands of dishes** until good cooking became instinct. That's **connectionist AI**, or neural networks: they soak up patterns from tons of examples. They shine at messy things like seeing and hearing, but they're a "black box" — hard to explain *why* they decided something. A newer approach, **neuro-symbolic AI**, tries to hire both cooks together for the best of each.
