question: "What is the ROC curve and AUC?"
topic: "Machine Learning"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

A yes/no classifier usually outputs a **score**, and you pick a cutoff above which you call something "positive." Slide that cutoff and you trade two things: catch more real positives, but also trigger more false alarms. The **ROC curve** simply **plots that trade-off across every possible cutoff** — true-positive rate up the side, false-alarm rate along the bottom. A model that hugs the top-left corner is great; a diagonal line is pure coin-flipping. **AUC** is just the **area under that curve**, boiled down to one number from 0 to 1: 1.0 is perfect, 0.5 is random guessing. It has a neat meaning — the chance the model scores a random true positive higher than a random true negative. One caveat: when positives are very rare, a precision-recall version often tells a more honest story than ROC-AUC.
