question: "Explain the minimax algorithm and alpha-beta pruning."
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Picture two rivals playing tic-tac-toe, thinking ahead. On your turn you want the highest score; your opponent, on theirs, wants the lowest. **Minimax** imagines the whole tree of future moves, assuming each side plays perfectly, then works backward: you pick moves that maximize your outcome, they pick moves that minimize it. That's a lot of branches to check. **Alpha-beta pruning** is a shortcut that skips branches you already know can't matter. Imagine you find one reply so bad for your opponent that they'd never allow it; you can stop examining that whole line, because a better option for them already exists. By tracking the best each side can guarantee so far, you chop away useless branches and reach the *same* best move as full minimax, just far faster, letting you think roughly twice as many moves ahead.
