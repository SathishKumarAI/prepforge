The real axis isn't "how messy" — it's **where the schema lives**, and that placement dictates cost, flexibility, and how you query.

- **Structured data** carries its schema *outside* the data, defined in advance: rows and columns with fixed types, like a SQL table. Because the shape is guaranteed before a byte arrives, you get fast indexed queries and strong integrity — at the price of rigidity. Changing shape means migrating the whole table.
- **Semi-structured data** carries its schema *inline*, alongside each record: JSON, XML, key-value pairs. Every object announces its own fields, so records can differ and evolve freely. The cost is that you must parse structure at read time and can't assume uniformity — "schema-on-read" rather than "schema-on-write."
- **Unstructured data** has *no explicit schema at all*: raw text, images, audio, video. The meaning is latent in the bytes and must be *extracted* (embeddings, models, feature detectors) before you can compute over it.

The unifying principle: you always pay the structuring cost — the only question is **when**. Structured pays it up front (write time), unstructured defers it entirely to analysis time, semi-structured splits the difference.

Non-obvious insight: this is exactly why the industry shifted from data *warehouses* (schema-on-write, ~80% of data is unstructured and won't fit) to data *lakes* (store raw, structure later). The volume of unstructured data made deferring the cost the only scalable option.
