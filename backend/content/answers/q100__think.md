question: "How do you communicate analytical findings to non-technical stakeholders and drive action?"
topic: "Data Analytics"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

1. **Clarify first.** Ask who the audience is, what decision they own, and how much time you have. State the assumption: the goal is a decision and an action, not a methods lecture.
2. **Mental model.** Lead with the answer, not the analysis. Structure like BLUF (bottom line up front) / the pyramid principle: conclusion first, then supporting evidence, then detail on demand.
3. **Reason step by step.** Open with the headline finding and its "so what" in business terms (dollars, users, risk). Translate metrics into outcomes stakeholders care about. Use one clear visual per point with the takeaway written on it. Quantify uncertainty plainly ("we're confident within a few percent") rather than with jargon. Then make a specific, owned recommendation with a next step — findings drive action only when someone knows exactly what to do. Keep methodology in an appendix for those who ask.
4. **Traps to avoid.** Data-dumping every chart you made; burying the conclusion; using p-values, model names, or SQL in the room; overclaiming certainty; and presenting insight with no recommendation.
5. **Sanity-check and deliver.** Test: could the exec repeat your one-sentence takeaway and the action afterward? Rehearse the headline out loud, anticipate the "why should I believe this?" question, and close by asking for the specific decision.
