question: "What is the difference between Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning?"

Think of three nested Russian dolls, one tucked inside the next. The biggest doll is **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: any way we make machines do things that normally need human smarts, like planning a chess move or following written rules. Open it up and inside is **Machine Learning (ML)**: instead of a programmer writing every rule by hand, the machine studies lots of examples and figures out the patterns itself. Open that doll and inside sits **Deep Learning (DL)**: a kind of ML that stacks many layers of tiny math units, called a neural network, to handle messy things like photos, sound, and language. So every deep-learning system is machine learning, and every machine-learning system is AI, but not the other way around. The dolls only fit together one direction.
