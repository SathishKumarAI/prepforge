question: "What is marketing attribution and what are the main attribution models?"

Attribution is about giving credit. Before someone buys, they usually touch several ads, a search, a social post, an email. Attribution decides how much credit each of those touches gets for the sale, so you know where to spend your budget. It's like awarding assists for a goal in soccer, who really set it up?

The simple models give all the credit to one touch: **first-touch** (the ad that first got their attention) or **last-touch** (the final click before buying). Both are easy but blind to the rest of the journey.

Fairer models spread it out: **linear** gives every touch equal credit, **time-decay** favors touches closer to the purchase, and **position-based** rewards the first and last most. The smartest, **data-driven**, uses math to estimate each touch's real added contribution.

The catch: correlation can mislead, so the gold standard for true impact is an actual experiment, showing some people an ad and holding others back.
