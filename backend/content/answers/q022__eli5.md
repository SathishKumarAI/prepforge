**Computer vision** is the field that teaches computers to "see" — to pull meaning out of photos and video the way your eyes and brain do. Think of a brand-new worker at a busy shipping warehouse. On day one they must learn several separate skills. First, glance at a box and name what's inside: that's **image classification** (one label for the whole picture). Next, point to *where* each item sits and draw a box around it: **object detection**. Then color in every single pixel by what it belongs to: **segmentation**. They also learn to read printed labels (**OCR**, optical character recognition), recognize faces, and judge how a person is standing (**pose estimation**). Modern systems learn all these skills from millions of examples using deep neural networks. The payoffs show up in self-driving cars, medical scans, and everyday photo apps.
