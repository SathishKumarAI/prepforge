question: "What makes an effective analytics dashboard?"

An effective dashboard answers a specific decision for a specific audience, fast. I'd frame it through **Customer Obsession** and **Insist on the Highest Standards**: I start by asking who uses this, what decision they make, and how often, then design backward from that. A dashboard that shows everything communicates nothing.

My principles: lead with the two or three KPIs that matter at the top, provide drill-down for the "why," use consistent and honest scales, show context (target, prior period, trend) not just a bare number, and make it load fast and refresh on a known cadence. I ruthlessly cut vanity metrics, that's **Invent and Simplify**.

A real example: I inherited a 30-widget executive dashboard nobody trusted or opened. I interviewed the three actual users, learned they made one weekly staffing decision, and rebuilt it in QuickSight around five KPIs with drill-downs. Weekly active usage went from near zero to 100% of the leadership team, and the staffing decision moved from gut-feel to data.

A bar-raiser listens for whether I design from the user's decision and take ownership of adoption and data trust, plus whether I quantify impact rather than just listing dashboard best practices.
