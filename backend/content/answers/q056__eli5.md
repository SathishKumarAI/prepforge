question: "What is the difference between structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data?"
topic: "Data Science"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Picture three ways your belongings might be stored. A neat **filing cabinet** with labeled folders and identical forms is **structured data**: everything sits in tidy rows and columns with a fixed layout, like a spreadsheet or database table — easy to search with SQL.

**Labeled moving boxes** — each tagged "kitchen" or "books" but packed loosely inside — are **semi-structured data**: there are organizing tags and markers, but no rigid template. Think JSON, XML, or YAML files, and emails. Flexible, and often kept in document databases.

A **pile of random stuff in the attic** is **unstructured data**: free text, images, audio, video, PDFs — no predefined organization at all. It's the majority of the world's data and needs specialized tools (NLP for text, computer vision for images) to make sense of it.

In practice, pipelines often convert messy semi- and unstructured data into structured features before analysis.
