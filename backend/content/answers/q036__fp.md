question: "What is the difference between L1 and L2 regularization?"

Both add a penalty on weight magnitude to the loss, shrinking coefficients to curb overfitting. The whole difference — and it's profound — comes from the **geometry of the penalty's constraint region.**

L2 penalizes the sum of *squared* weights; its constraint region is a sphere. L1 penalizes the sum of *absolute* values; its region is a diamond (an L1-ball with sharp corners on the axes). Minimizing the loss subject to a budget means finding where the loss contours first touch that region. A sphere is smooth, so contact happens generically at points with *all* coordinates nonzero — L2 shrinks weights *toward* zero but rarely *to* zero. The diamond has **corners on the axes**, and high-dimensional contours overwhelmingly touch corners — where some coordinates are *exactly* zero. That's why **L1 produces sparse solutions** (automatic feature selection) and L2 doesn't.

Reframed via the gradient: L1's penalty gradient is a constant ±λ regardless of weight size, so it pushes small weights all the way to zero. L2's is proportional to the weight, so the push vanishes as the weight shrinks — it never quite arrives.

Under a Bayesian lens: L2 = a Gaussian prior on weights, L1 = a Laplace prior (peaked at zero) — which is exactly why Laplace induces sparsity.

The non-obvious insight: L1 selects arbitrarily among *correlated* features (picks one, zeros the rest), while L2 spreads weight across them — Elastic Net combines both to get sparsity without that instability.
