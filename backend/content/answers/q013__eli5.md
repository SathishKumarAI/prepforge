Imagine hiring a brilliant but very literal new assistant who does exactly what you ask, no more, no less. Ask vaguely and you get a shrug; ask precisely and you get gold. **Prompt engineering** is the craft of writing that request, the input you give an AI model, so it gives you what you actually want. Because these models are super sensitive to wording, small changes matter a lot. Handy tricks include: state clear instructions and the exact format you want back, show a couple of examples of good answers, ask the model to 'think step by step' for tough reasoning, give it a role or context, and clearly separate your instructions from your data. It matters because it's the cheapest, fastest way to steer a general model toward a specific job, often good enough to skip expensive retraining, and it helps with accuracy and safety too.
