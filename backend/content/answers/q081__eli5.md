question: "What is a data warehouse and how does it differ from a database?"

A regular database is like the cash register in a busy shop: it handles one customer at a time, super fast, ringing up sales and updating stock the instant something is bought. A data warehouse is more like a giant archive room holding every receipt the shop ever printed, arranged so you can answer big questions like "how did sales change each December over five years?"

The everyday database (called **OLTP**, meaning it handles transactions) is built for many tiny reads and writes without mixing records up. The warehouse (called **OLAP**, meaning analysis) is built to scan and add up huge piles of history at once. To make that fast, warehouses store copies of the same info in fewer, wider tables instead of splitting everything apart. Examples: Snowflake, BigQuery. A **data lake** is the cheaper, messier cousin holding raw, unsorted stuff.
