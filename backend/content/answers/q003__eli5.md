question: "Explain the difference between supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning."
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"

---

Picture three ways a kid might learn. First, **supervised learning**: you hand them flashcards where each picture already has the answer on the back, so they practice matching 'this is a cat' until they can label brand-new pictures. The machine learns from examples that come with correct answers. Second, **unsupervised learning**: you dump a pile of unlabeled photos and just say 'sort these into groups.' Nobody says what the groups are; the machine finds hidden patterns on its own. Third, **reinforcement learning**: like training a puppy with treats and 'no,' the machine tries actions, gets rewards or penalties, and slowly learns which choices pay off over time. So the big difference is what feedback each one gets: full answers, no answers, or reward signals earned through trial and error.
