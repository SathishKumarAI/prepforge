These are two very different jobs done with data. Think of a busy shop.

**OLTP** (online transaction processing) is the cashier at the register. It handles a flood of tiny, fast actions all day, ring up a sale, update stock, record a return, one record at a time, without ever mixing customers up. Speed, many people at once, and getting each transaction exactly right are what matter. It powers banking, orders, and checkouts.

**OLAP** (online analytical processing) is the analyst in a back office studying years of those receipts. It runs big, slow questions that scan and add up millions of past records: "how did revenue trend by region last year?" It's built for heavy reading, not constant writing.

So OLTP handles today's live transactions; OLAP crunches piles of history. Data usually flows from the cashier systems into an analysis warehouse (via ETL) so big reports don't slow down the registers.
