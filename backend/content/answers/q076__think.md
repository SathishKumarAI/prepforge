1. **Clarify first.** Confirm the interviewer wants the canonical four-tier taxonomy, not tooling or data formats. Assume a business-analytics context where each tier answers a different question.
2. **Mental model.** Order them along a ladder of increasing sophistication *and* business value, each keyed to a question word: what happened, why, what will happen, what to do. That question mapping is your recall anchor.
3. **Reason step by step.** Descriptive — *what happened?* dashboards, KPIs, reports. Diagnostic — *why?* drill-down, root-cause, correlation. Predictive — *what's likely?* statistical/ML forecasting like churn or demand. Prescriptive — *what should we do?* optimization and simulation like dynamic pricing. Note they build on each other: reliable prediction needs solid descriptive foundations.
4. **Traps to dodge.** Do not offer decoy lists (data sizes, or SQL/Python/R/Excel) — those aren't analytics *types*. Do not scramble the order or merge diagnostic into descriptive.
5. **Sanity-check and communicate.** Verify you have exactly four, in ascending order, each tied to its question. Deliver the ladder and add the insight that most organizations start descriptive/diagnostic and mature toward predictive/prescriptive as value and complexity rise.
