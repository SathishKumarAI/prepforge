Imagine a student who, while studying, accidentally peeks at the actual exam answer key. They ace every practice test and feel brilliant — then bomb the real exam, because their "skill" was really just secret access to the answers. **Data leakage** is that peek happening to your model: information it won't have in real life sneaks into training, so validation scores look amazing but real-world performance collapses.

Common sneaky forms: a feature that's secretly the answer in disguise (like using an "account-closed date" to predict who will close their account); or fitting your scaler or imputer on the *whole* dataset before splitting, letting test-set statistics bleed into training.

Prevent it by **splitting first**, then learning all preprocessing on the training portion only — pipelines automate this per fold. Respect time order for time series, audit each feature's real-world availability, and treat suspiciously perfect scores as a warning, not a win.
