A **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** is a number that tells you whether you're hitting an important goal — like the gauges on a car's dashboard. Your car has hundreds of internal measurements, but the dashboard shows only the vital few: speed, fuel, engine temperature. Those are the ones that actually change how you drive.

A good KPI is often called **SMART**: **Specific** (clearly defined), **Measurable** (you have the data), **Achievable** (realistic), **Relevant** (tied to a real goal), and **Time-bound** (over a set period). Beyond that, it should be **actionable** — someone can actually influence it — and have a clear owner.

Beware "vanity metrics" — the shiny dial that looks impressive but tells you nothing useful for steering. It also helps to mix *leading* gauges that hint at the future with *lagging* ones that report the past. And don't cram the dashboard — too many KPIs and you watch none.
