question: "What is Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)?"

Imagine the difference between a closed-book exam, where you answer purely from memory and might misremember, and an open-book exam, where you look up the facts first. **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** turns an AI language model into an open-book test-taker. First, your documents are chopped into chunks and filed in a special library organized by *meaning*, not just keywords. When you ask a question, the system fetches the chunks whose meaning is closest to your question, pastes them into the prompt as reference material, and only then does the model write its answer, grounded in what it just looked up. The payoff is big: fewer made-up facts, the ability to cite sources, and fresh or private knowledge without retraining the whole model. The tricky parts are chopping documents up well and making sure the right passages actually get retrieved.
