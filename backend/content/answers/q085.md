The standard dimensions are **accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, validity, and uniqueness** — with **integrity** often added as a seventh.

Briefly:

- **Accuracy** — does the value reflect reality? A revenue figure that's off by a currency conversion bug is complete and valid but wrong.
- **Completeness** — are required fields and expected rows present? Missing `country` on 20% of users, or a pipeline that silently dropped a partition.
- **Consistency** — do the same facts agree across systems? Finance and product reporting different revenue is a consistency failure.
- **Timeliness** — is the data fresh enough for the decision? A daily dashboard on data that's three days stale is failing even if every value is accurate.
- **Validity** — does data conform to rules/formats? Emails match a pattern, ages are 0–120, enums are in the allowed set.
- **Uniqueness** — no unintended duplicates. Double-counted orders inflate every downstream metric.

In production, the ones that actually bite are **completeness** (silent partial loads) and **timeliness** (a late upstream job), because they fail quietly — the dashboard still renders, it's just wrong. That's why I instrument pipelines with automated checks: freshness SLAs, row-count anomaly detection, null-rate thresholds, and uniqueness assertions, using tools like dbt tests, Great Expectations, or Soda. The key operational move is **failing loud** — alert or block the pipeline rather than let bad data reach a dashboard, because a wrong number erodes trust in every number.
