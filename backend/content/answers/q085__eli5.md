Imagine grading a grocery delivery. "Good data" isn't one thing, it's several separate checks, just like inspecting your bags.

**Accuracy**: does the label match reality, are those really apples? **Completeness**: did everything you ordered arrive, or is the milk missing? **Consistency**: do every receipt and app agree on the same price, with no contradictions? **Timeliness**: is it fresh and available when you need it, not last week's leftovers? **Validity**: does it fit the expected form, a real email address, a date that isn't February 30th? **Uniqueness**: were you charged for the eggs once, not three times by accident? **Integrity**: do the connections hold, every order linking to a real customer?

Why bother? Garbage in, garbage out: bad data quietly leads to wrong decisions. Turning "good data" into these specific, checkable rules lets you actually audit and fix it, using validation, profiling, and monitoring.
