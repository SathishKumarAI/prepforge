question: "At a high level, how does a large language model (LLM) generate text?"

You know how your phone suggests the next word as you text? A **large language model (LLM)** is that same idea turned up to superhuman levels. At its core it does one thing: given all the words so far, predict the most likely next little chunk of text, called a token. To write a sentence it works one token at a time: look at everything written so far, produce odds for what comes next, pick one, add it, then repeat, looping until it decides to stop. It learned these odds by reading mountains of text and practicing 'guess the next word' billions of times, which is why it picks up grammar, facts, and style. Importantly, it has no lookup table of truth tucked inside; everything is baked fuzzily into its internal settings. That's exactly why it can sometimes state wrong things with total confidence.
