In a class photo where everyone's around average height, one person on stilts instantly grabs your eye — they don't fit the pattern. **Outliers** are data points like that: values sitting far away from the rest. They might be genuine rare cases, plain measurement errors, or important signals like fraud.

To spot them, one classic trick is the **IQR method**: find the middle 50% of your data (between the 25th and 75th percentiles) and flag anything more than 1.5 times that range beyond either end — exactly what a **box plot** draws. Another is the **z-score**: flag points more than about three standard deviations from the mean. For complex, multi-feature data, algorithms like Isolation Forest help.

Don't just delete them blindly. **Investigate first**: remove clear errors, cap or transform extreme-but-real values, or keep them — because sometimes the outlier is the whole point of your analysis.
