question: "What are the main approaches to feature selection?"

Imagine packing a suitcase for a trip — you want only the clothes that actually help, not everything you own. In machine learning, **feature selection** is picking the genuinely useful input columns and leaving out the clutter, which makes models faster, simpler, and less likely to memorize noise.

There are three ways to choose. **Filter methods** judge each item on its own with a quick rule — "does this scarf ever get worn?" — ignoring the model entirely; fast, but they miss how items combine. **Wrapper methods** actually try packing different outfits and see which suitcase performs best; accurate but slow. **Embedded methods** do the choosing *while* the model trains — some models naturally shrink useless features' influence down to zero.

The goal is to escape the "too many columns" curse, drop noisy or repeated ones, and help the model generalize. (Unlike PCA, this keeps the original columns rather than blending them.)
