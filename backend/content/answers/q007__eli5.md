question: "How does the A* search algorithm work?"
topic: "AI"

---

Think of your phone's map app picking a route. To judge each next step it adds two numbers: how far you've *already* traveled from the start, plus a quick straight-line guess of how far is *still* left to the destination. **A\* search** does exactly this, scoring every option by 'cost so far' plus 'estimated cost to go,' and always expanding whichever looks cheapest overall. It keeps a to-do list sorted by that combined score, repeatedly grabs the most promising spot, checks its neighbors, and updates their scores until it reaches the goal. The clever part is balance: counting distance already spent keeps it honest and optimal, while the guess about what's left keeps it fast so it won't explore pointless directions. As long as that guess never overestimates, A\* finds the shortest path. Drop the guess entirely and it becomes plain distance-only search.
