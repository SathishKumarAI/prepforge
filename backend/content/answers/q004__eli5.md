Think of a robot vacuum roaming your living room. It senses the world (bumping walls, spotting dirt), decides what to do, then acts (turning, sucking) to reach a goal: a clean floor. That sense-then-act loop is exactly what AI means by an **intelligent agent**, something that perceives its surroundings and takes actions to achieve goals. Agents come in levels of cleverness. The simplest just react to what's right in front of them, like 'wall ahead, turn.' Smarter ones keep a memory map of the room, aim at goals, or weigh trade-offs to pick the best option. The fanciest ones learn and improve with experience. A good agent is 'rational': it picks the action most likely to get the result it wants, given what it knows. Modern AI assistants that use tools and memory are this same idea, scaled way up.
