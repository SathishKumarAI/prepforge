Sharing analysis with non-technical people is like writing a news story, not a lab report. Journalists lead with the headline, the "so what," not the interview transcripts. Do the same: state your key insight and recommendation first, then the supporting detail for anyone who wants it. This is called **BLUF**, bottom line up front.

Speak their language: frame everything as business impact, money, risk, customers, time, not p-values or model internals. Wrap it in a simple story: here's the situation, here's the problem, here's the evidence, here's what I suggest we do. A clear narrative persuades far better than a wall of numbers.

Use clean, well-labeled charts that make the point obvious, be honest about uncertainty without drowning them in it, and tie the finding to a specific next step with an owner. Remember: the best insight is worthless if no one understands or trusts it enough to act.
