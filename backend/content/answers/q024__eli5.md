Think of an AI as a student who learns entirely from the textbooks you hand it. If those books mostly feature one kind of person, or repeat old unfair assumptions, the student absorbs those blind spots — and confidently repeats them. That's **bias** in AI, and it usually starts with the data. Common sources: the training data **under-represents** some groups; it came from a **skewed sample**; the human **labels** were inconsistent or prejudiced; or the model chases overall accuracy while quietly failing a subgroup. Feedback loops make it worse when predictions shape future data. To address it, give the student **broader, more representative material**, measure performance **per group** (not just overall), adjust the data or model to rebalance, document everything, and keep a human checking outcomes. Bias is never fully "solved" — it must be watched and managed continuously.
