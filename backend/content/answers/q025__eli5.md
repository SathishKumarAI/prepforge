question: "Explain temperature, top-k, and top-p (nucleus) sampling in LLM decoding."
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

When an LLM picks its next word, it doesn't get one answer — it gets a ranked list of options with probabilities, like a spinner wheel where bigger slices are likelier words. These three settings reshape that wheel. **Temperature** controls boldness: turn it *down* and the biggest slice swallows the wheel (safe, predictable); turn it *up* and slices even out (surprising, creative); near zero it always grabs the top word. **Top-k** says "only keep the k biggest slices, throw away the rest," trimming the long tail of weird words — but with a fixed count. **Top-p** (nucleus) is smarter: keep just enough top slices to cover, say, 90% of the probability, so the pool shrinks when the model is confident and widens when it's unsure. Low settings suit facts; higher ones suit storytelling.
