Think of a data science project like putting on a dinner for guests. First you decide **what meal to serve and why** — the business problem and how you'll measure success. Then you **gather ingredients** (collect data) and **wash and chop** them (clean and prepare) — usually the messiest, most time-consuming part. Next you **taste and explore** the ingredients to understand what you've got (exploratory analysis) and **prep special components** (feature engineering).

Then you actually **cook** (train models), **taste-test against a standard** (evaluate on held-out data), and finally **serve it to guests** (deploy to production). Even after serving, you **watch reactions and tweak the recipe** (monitor performance and retrain as the world shifts).

The key insight: it's **iterative, not a straight line**. A surprise during tasting often sends you back to re-chop ingredients. And throughout, you keep talking to the guests — communicating results to stakeholders.
