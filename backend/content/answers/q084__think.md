question: "What makes an effective analytics dashboard?"
topic: "Data Analytics"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

1. **Clarify first.** Ask who the audience is (executive vs operational analyst) and what decision the dashboard supports. State the assumption: a dashboard exists to drive action, not to display everything available.
2. **Mental model.** Think "purpose → audience → key questions → metrics → layout." Design backward from the decisions people make, not forward from the data you happen to have.
3. **Reason step by step.** Effective dashboards: lead with a few north-star metrics; show context (trend, target, prior period) not just a bare number; follow a visual hierarchy (most important top-left); use consistent, restrained chart types; allow drill-down for detail without cluttering the summary; and refresh at a cadence that matches the decision speed.
4. **Traps to avoid.** Vanity metrics with no action attached; cramming 30 charts so nothing stands out; missing comparison baselines (a number with no target or trend is meaningless); rainbow color abuse; and dashboards nobody defined an owner or question for.
5. **Sanity-check and deliver.** The test: can a viewer answer "is this good or bad, and what should I do?" within seconds? Communicate by stating the guiding principle — clarity and decision-support over completeness — then list the concrete traits (context, hierarchy, restraint).
