question: "How do you handle missing data?"

Imagine a stack of survey forms where some people left boxes blank. Before analyzing, you must decide what to do with those gaps — and the right move depends on **why** they're blank. Did people skip randomly, or did, say, high earners deliberately hide their income? That reason (statisticians label the cases MCAR, MAR, and MNAR) shapes your choice.

You have two broad options. **Deletion**: drop the incomplete forms or a mostly-empty question — fine if only a few are missing at random, wasteful otherwise. Or **imputation**: fill the gaps in — with a simple average, with the previous value for time series, or with a smarter model that predicts the missing value from the other answers.

Two best practices: learn any fill-in values from your **training data only** (or you leak information), and often add a "was-missing" flag — because the very fact that something is blank can itself be a useful clue.
