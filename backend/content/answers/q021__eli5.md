question: "Why are Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) well-suited for images?"
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Imagine trying to spot faces in a huge crowd photo. You wouldn't stare at all million pixels at once. Instead you'd use one small "pattern stamp" — say, one that lights up when it sees an eye — and slide it across every part of the picture. A **CNN** (convolutional neural network) works exactly like this. Each filter is a small stamp that scans little patches looking for one simple thing: an edge, a color, a texture. Because the *same* stamp is reused everywhere, the network learns far fewer settings and spots a feature no matter where it sits. Early layers catch edges; later layers combine those into eyes, then whole faces. A "pooling" step then shrinks the image, keeping the gist while ignoring tiny shifts. That efficient, position-friendly design is why CNNs excel at images.
