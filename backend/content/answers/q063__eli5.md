question: "How would you design an A/B test?"

Suppose you run a bakery and wonder if a new cookie recipe sells better. You don't just switch — you randomly hand some customers the old cookie (group A) and some the new one (group B), then see which gets bought more. That random splitting is the whole trick: it makes the two groups otherwise similar, so any difference in sales is caused by the recipe, not by luck about who walked in.

To design it well: pick one clear success measure (say, purchase rate), and figure out how many customers you need *in advance* so you can actually detect a real difference. Run it long enough to cover normal ups and downs, and don't peek and stop early the moment it looks good. Finally, check the numbers truly beat chance — and watch that nothing else quietly got worse.
