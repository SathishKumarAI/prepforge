1. **Clarify first.** Frame this as a paradigm contrast, not a "which is better" - assume the interviewer wants the trade-off axes plus historical context. Signal you'll also mention where the field is heading.

2. **Mental model.** Compare along fixed axes: knowledge source (hand-coded rules vs learned from data), representation (explicit symbols vs distributed vectors), strengths, and weaknesses. A parallel table in your head keeps the answer balanced.

3. **Reason step by step.** Symbolic AI (GOFAI) encodes knowledge as symbols, logic, and rules - think expert systems. It's interpretable and strong at reasoning/planning but brittle and needs manual knowledge engineering. Connectionist AI learns sub-symbolic distributed representations from examples - great at perception and generalization, but a data-hungry black box weak at explicit logic. So one is top-down and rule-driven, the other bottom-up and data-driven.

4. **Traps to avoid.** Don't reverse them (symbolic does NOT learn from data). Don't imply neural nets can't handle images - that's their strength. Don't present them as mutually exclusive.

5. **Sanity-check.** Close by naming neuro-symbolic AI as the synthesis (neural perception + symbolic reasoning), which proves you see beyond the binary and communicate why it matters.
