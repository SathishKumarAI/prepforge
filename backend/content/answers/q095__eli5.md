Finding the second-highest salary is like finding the runner-up in a race, and the tricky part is ties at the top.

The safest way: first find the very highest salary, then ask for the highest salary that is strictly below it.

```sql
SELECT MAX(salary)
FROM employees
WHERE salary < (SELECT MAX(salary) FROM employees);
```

Because it looks for the top value *under* the winner, it handles several people sharing the top salary, and politely returns nothing (NULL) if there's no second value.

A modern way uses **DENSE_RANK**, which numbers salaries from highest down and lets tied people share a rank, then you pick rank 2.

Watch out for the tempting shortcut "sort descending, skip one, take the next" (`LIMIT 1 OFFSET 1`). If two people tie for first, it just hands you a copy of the top salary, not the real runner-up. Prefer the subquery or DENSE_RANK.
