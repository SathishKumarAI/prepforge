question: "Explain precision, recall, and F1 score. When would you prioritize each?"
topic: "Machine Learning"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Imagine a **fishing net meant to catch only tuna**. **Precision** asks: of everything you hauled in, what fraction was actually tuna? Low precision means your bucket is full of accidental junk fish (false alarms). **Recall** asks the opposite: of all the tuna in the sea, how many did you actually catch? Low recall means lots of tuna slipped through (missed cases). The catch is they trade off — cast a huge greedy net and you catch every tuna (high recall) but scoop tons of junk (low precision). The **F1 score** blends both into one balanced number. Which to favor depends on cost: prize **precision** when false alarms are expensive (flagging a real email as spam); prize **recall** when misses are dangerous (missing a cancer or a fraud case). F1 is your go-to when classes are lopsided and you need one fair summary.
