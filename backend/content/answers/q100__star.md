question: "How do you communicate analytical findings to non-technical stakeholders and drive action?"
topic: "Data Analytics"

**Situation** I'd spent two weeks on a churn model and presented it to our VP of Sales with confusion matrices and ROC curves. His eyes glazed over, he asked "so what do I do Monday?", and the meeting ended with no decision. I'd delivered analysis but not action.

**Task** I needed to re-communicate the same findings so a non-technical leader would act on them.

**Action** I rebuilt the story around his decisions, not my methodology. I led with the headline: "We can flag the 200 accounts most likely to churn this quarter, worth $1.2M." I used the pyramid principle, putting the recommendation first and the supporting detail underneath for anyone who wanted it. I swapped the ROC curve for a plain bar chart of at-risk revenue by segment, translated model precision into "8 of every 10 accounts we flag really are at risk," and ended with three concrete next steps and an owner for each. I dropped every piece of jargon.

**Result** He approved a retention campaign in that meeting, and it saved about 15% of the flagged revenue that quarter. My lesson: stakeholders act on a clear recommendation tied to money and a next step, not on model metrics.
