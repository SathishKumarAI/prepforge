question: "What are the key components of a time series, and what is seasonality vs. trend?"

A time series is just measurements laid out in time order, like daily sales. To understand it, we split it into layers, a bit like watching the ocean.

The **trend** is the tide: the slow, long-term direction, sales drifting upward year after year. **Seasonality** is the regular waves: predictable ups and downs on a fixed schedule, like retail spiking every December or traffic dipping every weekend. There's also a **cyclical** part, longer swells that don't follow the calendar neatly (economic booms and busts), and finally **noise**, the random choppiness you can't explain.

You combine these by adding them up, or by multiplying when the seasonal swings grow bigger as the total grows. Why separate them? So you don't mistake a normal December spike for real growth. Stripping out seasonality reveals the true underlying trend, which is exactly what you need to forecast honestly.
