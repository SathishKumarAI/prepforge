question: "What is the difference between Pearson and Spearman correlation?"
topic: "Data Science"

---

Both Pearson and Spearman answer "when one thing goes up, does the other tend to go up too?", giving a score from −1 to +1. The difference is *how strict* they are about the shape of that relationship.

**Pearson** looks at the actual values and only rewards a straight-line link — like checking whether points fall neatly along a ruler. It assumes fairly nice, bell-shaped data and gets thrown off by a single wild outlier.

**Spearman** ignores the raw numbers and instead ranks everything (1st, 2nd, 3rd...) then compares those ranks. So it just asks "do they climb together in order?", even if the climb curves rather than being straight. That makes it tougher against outliers and happy with ranked or lopsided data.

Rule of thumb: straight-line, well-behaved numbers → Pearson; curved-but-consistent, ranked, or messy data → Spearman. Neither one proves that one thing *causes* the other.
