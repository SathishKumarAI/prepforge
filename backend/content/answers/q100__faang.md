**Clarify** — I'll confirm the audience, the decision they own, and the forum (email, deck, dashboard), since these shape depth and format.

**Approach** — Lead with the answer, tie it to their goals, support with minimal visuals, and end on a concrete recommendation — not a data dump.

**Depth** — I use the **BLUF** (bottom-line-up-front) / pyramid structure: state the key insight and recommended action first, then the supporting evidence for those who want it. I translate metrics into business language and money ("checkout drop-off is costing ~$X/month") rather than jargon like p-values or query logic. Visuals carry one message each, with the takeaway written as the chart title. I quantify impact and the expected outcome of acting, acknowledge uncertainty honestly, and always close with a clear, owned next step. Tailoring — an executive gets three bullets; an analyst peer gets methodology — keeps it relevant.

**Edge cases** — Overloading slides, burying the lede, presenting correlation as causation, hiding assumptions, and no clear ask (so nothing happens). Hostile or skeptical audiences need the caveats surfaced proactively.

**Optimize & communicate** — I'd pre-align with the key decision-maker before the meeting, anticipate objections, and make the recommendation the easiest thing to say yes to. Framing communication as driving a *decision*, not reporting numbers, is exactly the influence-and-clarity signal interviewers score for senior analytics roles.
