Read the sentence 'The trophy didn't fit in the suitcase because *it* was too big.' To know what 'it' means, each word has to glance at the others. **Self-attention** is how a transformer lets every word do exactly that: look around at all the other words and decide which ones matter for its own meaning. Picture a room full of people where each person quickly asks everyone else a question, listens for who gives the most relevant answer, and updates their understanding based mostly on those. In the model, each word sends out a little 'what am I looking for' signal and a 'what I offer' signal; words that match get more weight. The result is that every word becomes context-aware. Because all words can check each other at once, instead of reading strictly left to right, transformers are fast and great at connecting distant words.
