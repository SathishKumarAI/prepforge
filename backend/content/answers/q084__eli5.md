question: "What makes an effective analytics dashboard?"
topic: "Data Analytics"

A good analytics dashboard works like the dashboard in your car. At a glance it shows the few things that actually matter, speed, fuel, a warning light, so you can drive without studying a manual.

To build one that works, first know who's looking: a CEO wants the big picture; a shift manager wants live detail. Put the most important numbers top-left, where eyes land first. Never show a number alone, always give context: is it up or down versus last month, or versus the goal? A lonely "1,200" tells you nothing.

Keep it clean: plenty of empty space, no clutter, the right chart for each metric, and consistent colors. Add filters or drill-downs only where they help. And make sure the data is trustworthy and fresh, with a clear "last updated" time. A dashboard should instantly answer: how are we doing, and what needs attention?
