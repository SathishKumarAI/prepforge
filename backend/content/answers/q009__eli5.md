question: "What is a knowledge graph?"
topic: "AI"

Picture a giant bulletin board covered in labeled dots connected by strings. One dot says 'Paris,' another says 'France,' and the string between them is labeled 'capital of.' That web of dots-and-strings is a **knowledge graph**: facts stored as little three-part statements, a thing, a relationship, and another thing. Because everything is linked, a computer can walk the strings to answer questions and even deduce facts nobody typed in directly, like realizing that if Paris is in France and France is in Europe, then Paris is in Europe. This structure lets machines reason over connected facts instead of just storing loose text. It powers search engines that show info boxes, recommendation systems, and question answering. It's also used to feed reliable facts to AI language models so they make fewer things up. The real strength is those meaningful, followable connections.
