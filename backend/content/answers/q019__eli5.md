question: "What is a Markov Decision Process (MDP)?"
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"

---

Think of a board game. You're on some square (a **state**), you choose a move (an **action**), a dice roll sends you somewhere new (with some **probability**), and you collect points along the way (a **reward**). A **Markov Decision Process (MDP)** is just the math version of that setup: states, possible actions, the odds of where each action lands you, the rewards, and a knob that says how much you care about future points versus points right now. There's one key rule: where you go next depends only on your *current* square and move, not on the whole history of how you got there. The goal is to find a **policy**, a game plan telling you the best move in every square, to rack up the most points over time. Working out the long-term value of each square is the heart of reinforcement learning.
