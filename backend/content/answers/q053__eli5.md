Before a detective names a suspect, they walk the crime scene: noting what's there, what's missing, what looks odd, and what connects to what. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** is that first walk-through of your dataset — investigating and summarizing it *before* you build any model, so you understand what you're actually working with.

You compute **summary statistics** (averages, spreads, counts), and you *look* — histograms and box plots to see how single variables are distributed, scatter plots and correlation matrices to see how pairs relate. Along the way you hunt for **missing values, outliers, duplicates, and strange data types**.

The goal is to build intuition, catch data-quality problems early, form hypotheses, and decide which features and models make sense. It's highly **visual and iterative**. Skipping it is how you end up with "garbage in, garbage out" — a polished model resting on flawed data nobody bothered to inspect.
