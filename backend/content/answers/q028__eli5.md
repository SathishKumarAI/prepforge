question: "Why do we split data into training, validation, and test sets?"
topic: "Machine Learning"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Imagine studying for a driving test. You practice on a **practice course** (the training set) until your skills sink in. But how do you know you're truly ready and not just good at *that one* course? You try a **different practice course** to tune your habits and pick your approach — that's the **validation set**, used to adjust settings and catch memorizing. Finally comes the **real exam on roads you've never seen** — the **test set** — taken exactly once to measure true ability. Here's the key rule: if you'd secretly practiced on the exam route beforehand, your score would be fake — flatteringly high but meaningless. That's why the test set stays sealed until the very end. When data is scarce, people rotate folds (cross-validation) instead. And always learn your prep steps from training data only, so exam info never leaks in.
