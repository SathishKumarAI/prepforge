question: "What is a heuristic in AI search, and what makes one admissible?"
topic: "AI"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Suppose you're driving to a friend's house and want a quick gut-estimate of how far is left. You glance at the straight-line, as-the-crow-flies distance. Real roads curve, so the actual drive is always *at least* that far, never shorter. That kind of educated guess about remaining cost is a **heuristic**, a rule of thumb that points a search in a promising direction so it doesn't wander everywhere. A heuristic is called **admissible** when it never *over*-guesses, staying optimistic like that crow-flies distance. Why does that matter? If your estimate never claims the goal is farther than it really is, smart search methods like A* are guaranteed to find the truly shortest path. Guess too high and you might wrongly skip the best route. So the safe, path-guaranteeing rule is simple: always underestimate, never overestimate.
