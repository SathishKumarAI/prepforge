**Clarify** — Confirm they want the standard analytics maturity ladder: descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive. Ask if they'd like a concrete domain (e.g., e-commerce) to ground each.

**Approach** — Define each type as answering a distinct question, in increasing order of complexity and business value.

**Depth** — **Descriptive** answers "What happened?" — aggregations, dashboards, KPIs summarizing past data (last month's revenue). **Diagnostic** answers "Why did it happen?" — drill-downs, correlations, root-cause and cohort analysis (why churn spiked). **Predictive** answers "What is likely to happen?" — statistical and ML models forecasting future outcomes (which users will churn). **Prescriptive** answers "What should we do about it?" — optimization, simulation, and recommendation engines that suggest actions (which retention offer to send). Each stage adds complexity, requires more data maturity, and delivers more value.

**Edge cases** — Skipping stages fails: prescriptive advice built on unreliable predictions misleads; predictive models are only as good as clean descriptive data. Prescriptive systems also need feedback loops and guardrails.

**Optimize & communicate** — Most organizations should master descriptive and diagnostic before investing in predictive/prescriptive. Narrate the ladder as increasing sophistication and value: "You earn the right to prescribe only once you can reliably predict." That structured progression is the signal.
