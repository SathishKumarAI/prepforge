question: "Explain the difference between INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, and FULL OUTER joins in SQL."
topic: "Data Analytics"

Imagine two lists: a list of **customers** and a list of their **orders**. A **join** stitches them together by a shared column (the customer's ID). The four join types differ only in which unmatched rows they keep.

**INNER JOIN** keeps only perfect matches — customers who actually placed an order. No order? You're dropped from the result.

**LEFT JOIN** keeps *every* customer, matched or not. A customer with zero orders still appears, with blanks (NULLs) where the order details would go.

**RIGHT JOIN** is the mirror image: keep every order, even one somehow missing a customer.

**FULL OUTER JOIN** keeps *everyone* from both lists, filling blanks wherever there's no match on either side.

A handy trick: a LEFT JOIN with a filter for "where the order is blank" finds customers who never ordered anything. Picking the wrong join is a classic way to accidentally drop or duplicate rows, so choose deliberately.
