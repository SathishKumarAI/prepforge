question: "What are SQL window functions and how do they differ from GROUP BY?"

Imagine a classroom of students, each with a test score. **GROUP BY** is like the teacher erasing everyone and writing just one summary line per group: "Class average: 78." The individual students vanish.

A **window function** is different. It keeps every student in their seat, but writes an extra note on each one's paper: "your class average is 78, and you rank 3rd." Nobody disappears, you just gain new columns calculated from the group around you.

You control the "window" with two ideas. **PARTITION BY** splits students into groups (say, by classroom) without collapsing them. **ORDER BY** sorts them inside the group, which is what lets you rank or build running totals. Common tricks: RANK, ROW_NUMBER, and LAG (peeking at the previous row).

So the rule: GROUP BY squashes rows into summaries; window functions add summary info while keeping every row, perfect for rankings and running totals.
