Imagine coaching someone to give better answers not with a rulebook, but by showing them pairs of responses and repeatedly saying 'this one's better than that one.' Over time they absorb your taste. **Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)** trains an AI language model that way, in three steps. First, show the model good example answers and have it copy the style. Second, have people rank several of the model's answers from best to worst, and use those rankings to train a separate 'judge' that scores any answer the way humans would. Third, let the model practice, rewarding it for answers the judge scores highly, while gently penalizing it for drifting too far from its original self so it doesn't learn weird tricks to game the score. The result is a model that's more helpful, honest, and harmless, though all that human ranking is costly.
