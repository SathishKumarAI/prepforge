**ETL** stands for **Extract, Transform, Load** — the three steps for moving data from scattered sources into one home like a data warehouse. Think of cooking a big meal. First you **extract**: gather raw ingredients from the fridge, garden, and store. Then you **transform**: wash, chop, and season everything. Finally you **load**: plate it up in the dining room, ready to eat.

**ELT** just swaps the last two steps — Extract, *Load*, then *Transform*. You haul all the raw ingredients straight into a giant, powerful kitchen first, then do the chopping and seasoning right there.

Why the difference? Old-style warehouses had modest "kitchens," so you prepped ingredients beforehand (ETL). Modern cloud warehouses are enormous and cheap to run, so it's easier to dump everything in raw and transform inside them (ELT) — plus you keep the untouched originals for later. Same goal: analysis-ready data; different place to do the prep.
