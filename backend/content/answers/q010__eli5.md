**Natural Language Processing (NLP)** is the part of AI that teaches computers to work with human language, the messy words we speak and type, rather than tidy numbers. Think of it like teaching a foreign exchange student to read, understand, and reply in a new language: lots of small skills add up to fluency. Those skills are the common NLP tasks. Splitting a sentence into individual words or word-pieces is one. Spotting names, places, and dates in a paragraph is another. Judging whether a review sounds happy or angry is 'sentiment analysis.' Translating between languages, summarizing a long article, and answering questions are more. Older methods mostly counted words, but today's NLP is powered by 'transformer' models, the same tech behind modern chatbots, which learn the meaning of words from their surrounding context by reading enormous amounts of text.
