**Slowly Changing Dimensions** are dimension attributes that change occasionally over time — a customer's address, a product's category, a sales rep's region — and SCD "types" are the standard strategies for handling those changes in a warehouse without corrupting historical facts.

The common types:

- **Type 0 — retain original.** Never update; the value is fixed (e.g., original signup date). Rare but valid.
- **Type 1 — overwrite.** Update in place, keep no history. Simple, but you *lose the past*: yesterday's report now reflects today's value. Fine for correcting errors, dangerous for real changes.
- **Type 2 — add a new row.** Insert a new record for the changed entity with a new surrogate key and `valid_from` / `valid_to` dates (plus an `is_current` flag). This **preserves full history** and is the workhorse — it lets a fact joined at transaction time attribute correctly (an order made while the customer lived in Texas stays attributed to Texas).
- **Type 3 — add a new column.** Keep a `previous_value` alongside `current_value`. Only remembers one prior state; used when you need limited before/after comparison.

Type 2 is what interviewers really want you to know. The key mechanic is the **surrogate key** — facts point at the surrogate, not the natural business key, so each historical version is a distinct dimension row. The classic bug in production is joining facts on the natural key instead of the surrogate, which silently collapses all versions and re-attributes old orders to the customer's current attributes. Types 4 (history table) and 6 (hybrid 1+2+3) exist but come up less often.
