question: "What is feature engineering and why is it important?"

**Feature engineering** is prepping your raw ingredients before cooking. A model, like a cook, can only work with what's on the counter — hand it a whole unpeeled onion and muddy potatoes and the dish suffers. So you **chop and season**: turn a raw date into "day of week" or "is it a holiday," scale numbers so big ones don't overpower small ones, turn text categories into tidy columns the model understands, combine two columns into a telling ratio, and fill in or flag missing values. Why bother? Especially for classic (non-deep-learning) models, **good features often beat a fancier algorithm** — the model can only learn from what your features actually reveal. Deep learning discovers some of this on its own, but thoughtful features still win on spreadsheet-style data. One caution: never build a feature using future or answer information — that's cheating (leakage).
