question: "What is overfitting and how can you prevent it?"
topic: "Machine Learning"
model: "Claude Code (authored)"
sources: []
---

Imagine a student who preps for an exam by **memorizing last year's answer key word-for-word** instead of understanding the subject. On those exact practice questions they're perfect — but hand them a fresh test and they flop, because they memorized noise, not the real pattern. That's **overfitting**: a model that nails its training data yet fails on new data. The giveaway is a big gap between great training scores and poor scores on held-out data. To prevent it, you make the model learn the *idea* rather than the answer key: give it **more (or augmented) data**, use **regularization** or **dropout** to discourage over-reliance on quirks, keep the model **simpler**, stop training early before memorizing kicks in, and check honestly with **cross-validation**. The opposite mistake, **underfitting**, is being too simple to learn even the basics. You want the balance between them.
