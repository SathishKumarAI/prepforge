question: "How does Q-learning work?"

Imagine learning a new video game with no manual. You try a move, see what happens, and jot a rough score in a cheat-sheet for 'in this situation, this move is worth about *this* much.' Over many tries you keep nudging those scores toward the truth. **Q-learning** does exactly that. For every situation-and-move pair it keeps an estimate of the long-term payoff, then updates it using what it just experienced: the immediate reward plus the best score available from wherever it landed. Little by little, those estimates settle onto the real values, and the best move in any spot is simply the highest-scoring one. Cleverly, it can learn the *optimal* strategy even while it's still poking around randomly to explore. When there are too many situations to list in a table, a neural network stands in for the cheat-sheet, giving Deep Q-Networks.
