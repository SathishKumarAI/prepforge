question: "What is a KPI and what makes a good one?"
topic: "Data Analytics"

---

1. **Clarify first.** Split the question into two: define KPI, then list qualities of a *good* one. Assume a business context and note a KPI must tie to a strategic objective, not just be any number you can compute.
2. **Mental model.** A KPI is strategy made measurable — it translates a goal into a tracked target. Use the **SMART** framework as your scaffold for "what makes it good."
3. **Reason step by step.** Define: a quantifiable metric of progress toward a key objective (MRR, churn rate, conversion). Then run SMART — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Layer on extra qualities: actionable (someone can move it), owned, and not a vanity metric. Distinguish leading indicators (predict, e.g. pipeline) from lagging ones (report, e.g. revenue).
4. **Traps to dodge.** Do not call it just "any database column" or a chart type. Do not praise vanity metrics that look good but drive no decision. Do not propose so many KPIs that focus dissolves — track the vital few.
5. **Sanity-check and communicate.** Pressure-test a candidate KPI: is it actionable, owned, and tied to a goal? If not, it fails. Present the definition, the SMART criteria, and the leading-vs-lagging nuance to show depth.
