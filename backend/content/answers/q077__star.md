question: "What is a KPI and what makes a good one?"

**Situation** A product team I supported was tracking 60-plus "KPIs" on a wall of dashboards, and nobody could say which ones mattered or whether we were winning.

**Task** I needed to cut the noise and define a small set of KPIs that actually drove decisions.

**Action** I defined what makes a good KPI, essentially SMART: it must tie directly to a business objective, be measurable and quantifiable, have an owner and a target, and be actionable so a change in it prompts a decision. I separated true KPIs from vanity metrics, page views looked nice but drove nothing, and from operational metrics that were just diagnostics. Working with the PM, we landed on five: weekly active users, activation rate, net revenue retention, gross margin, and NPS, each with an owner and a quarterly target. I also distinguished leading from lagging indicators so we had early signals, not just outcomes.

**Result** The focused scorecard became the weekly leadership review, and decisions got noticeably faster because everyone stared at the same five numbers. My lesson: a good KPI isn't just measurable, it changes behavior when it moves.
