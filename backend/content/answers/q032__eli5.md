question: "What is gradient descent?"

**Gradient descent** is how a model teaches itself, and it's just like **hiking down to the lowest valley in thick fog**. You can't see the whole landscape, but you *can* feel which way the ground slopes under your feet. So you take a small step downhill, feel again, step again — repeating until you can't go lower. Here the "landscape" is the model's error, and each step nudges the model's settings to make mistakes a little smaller. The **learning rate** is your step size: too big and you leap past the valley or bounce around; too small and you crawl forever. There are flavors: use the *whole* map each step (batch), one random spot (stochastic), or a small handful (mini-batch, the usual pick). Smarter versions like **Adam** adjust step size and add momentum, like a ball rolling downhill picking up speed.
