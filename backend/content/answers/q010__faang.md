**Clarify** — I'll confirm the interviewer wants a definition plus a well-organized task inventory, and I'll group tasks rather than list them randomly to show structure.

**Approach** — Define NLP, then organize common tasks by category (understanding, generation, structure), with a note on how the field evolved.

**Depth** — NLP is the field enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language—bridging unstructured text/speech and machine-processable meaning. Common tasks by category: *Classification/understanding*—sentiment analysis, spam detection, intent classification, topic modeling. *Sequence labeling*—named entity recognition (NER), part-of-speech tagging. *Structure/parsing*—syntactic and dependency parsing, coreference resolution. *Generation*—machine translation, summarization, question answering, dialogue. *Retrieval*—semantic search, information extraction. Underlying building blocks include tokenization, embeddings, and language modeling.

**Edge cases** — Language is ambiguous (word sense, sarcasm, negation), context-dependent, and multilingual with low-resource languages. Idioms, typos, code-switching, and domain jargon break naive models. Evaluation is tricky—BLEU/ROUGE imperfectly capture generation quality.

**Optimize & communicate** — I'd note the shift from rule-based and statistical methods to transformer-based LLMs that now handle many tasks with one pretrained model plus prompting or fine-tuning. Signaling that I can both categorize tasks *and* explain the paradigm shift—rather than reciting a flat list—demonstrates structured thinking and current awareness.
