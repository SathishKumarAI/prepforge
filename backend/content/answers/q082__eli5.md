Picking a chart is like choosing the right tool for a job: you wouldn't hammer a screw. The shape of your question decides the tool, not your mood.

Comparing separate categories, like sales per city? Use **bars**. Showing how something changes over time? A **line**, because your eye follows the slope like a road. Showing pieces of a whole? A **stacked bar** (a pie only if there are just two or three slices). Checking whether two numbers move together, like height and weight? A **scatter plot**, one dot per thing. Showing how one number spreads out? A **histogram**.

The golden rules: keep it simple and honest. Start bar axes at zero so nothing looks exaggerated, label everything clearly, and skip 3D effects and decoration. A good chart makes the point obvious before anyone reads a single word.
