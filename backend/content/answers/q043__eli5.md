question: "What is feature scaling and which algorithms need it?"

Imagine measuring how "far apart" two people are using both their height in meters and their salary in dollars. Salaries are huge (tens of thousands) while heights are tiny (around 1.7). Plug both into a distance calculation and salary completely drowns out height — not because it matters more, but simply because its numbers are bigger. **Feature scaling** fixes this by putting every feature on a comparable range, so each gets a fair say.

Two common ways: **standardization** rescales so a feature averages 0 with a typical spread of 1; **min-max** squeezes values into a 0-to-1 band.

Algorithms that measure distances or ride gradients need it: k-NN, k-means, SVM, PCA, and neural networks. Tree-based models — decision trees, random forests, boosting — don't, because they split on thresholds, so the raw scale never matters. Always fit the scaler on training data only.
