A cohort is just a group that started together, like everyone who graduated high school in 2020. **Cohort analysis** follows each starting group over time instead of blending everyone into one blurry average.

Picture a table: each row is a "class" (say, users who signed up in January), and the columns are months since they joined, month 0, 1, 2, and so on. Each cell shows something like what fraction are still active. Reading across a row shows how that group fades or sticks.

Why it's powerful: total users can keep rising while people quietly leak out the back, and a single average hides that. Cohorts expose the leak. They also let you check whether newer signups behave better than older ones, which tells you if a product change actually helped. That's why apps and subscription businesses live by cohort retention charts.
