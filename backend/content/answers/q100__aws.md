I'd frame this through **Customer Obsession** (the stakeholder is my customer) and **Deliver Results**, because analysis that doesn't change a decision delivered nothing. My approach: **lead with the answer and the recommended action**, not the methodology. I start with the "so what," the one insight and what we should do, then support it, and keep the statistical machinery in an appendix.

I translate metrics into business language (dollars, customers, risk), use one clear visual per point, anticipate the "why should I trust this," and always end with a specific, owned next step. I tailor depth to the audience and invite the decision explicitly.

A real example: I found that a checkout bug was costing an estimated $200K per quarter. Instead of a 30-slide analysis, I opened a leadership review with one slide: "We're losing ~$200K a quarter to one mobile checkout bug; fixing it is a two-day change, here's the funnel evidence." The VP approved the fix on the spot; we recovered roughly $180K annualized and I tracked the result to confirm it landed.

A bar-raiser listens for whether I lead with the decision and quantified impact, take ownership of driving action rather than just presenting, and adapt to a non-technical audience, closing the loop by measuring the outcome.
