You've got a favorite restaurant you know is good, and a brand-new one just opened. Do you order the safe favorite, or gamble on the newcomer that *might* be even better? That everyday dilemma is the **exploration-exploitation trade-off**. Exploitation means cashing in on what you already know works. Exploration means trying uncertain options to gather information that could pay off later. Lean too hard on your favorite and you might never discover something better, getting stuck in a rut. Explore too much and you waste meals on duds. Balancing both earns the most satisfaction over the long run. AI systems handle this with simple recipes: mostly pick the best-known option but occasionally roll the dice, explore a lot early then settle down later, or deliberately favor the options you're most unsure about. This same puzzle shows up whenever you must learn and earn at the same time.
