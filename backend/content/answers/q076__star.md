**Situation** I joined a struggling analytics team whose dashboards only ever answered "what happened last month," and the VP was frustrated they never helped anyone decide anything.

**Task** I was asked to level up our analytics maturity and give the org a shared vocabulary for what different analyses actually deliver.

**Action** I introduced the four types of analytics as a ladder. Descriptive answers what happened, our existing revenue and traffic dashboards. Diagnostic answers why it happened, so I added cohort and drill-down analysis to explain a churn spike. Predictive answers what will happen, where I built a forecasting model for next-quarter demand. Prescriptive answers what we should do about it, so I layered in an optimization that recommended inventory allocation given the forecast. I mapped each existing report to a rung and showed where the gaps were.

**Result** Within a quarter we moved two flagship dashboards from purely descriptive to diagnostic-plus-predictive, and the demand forecast cut overstock by about 12%. The framework gave stakeholders a clear way to ask for the level of insight they actually needed instead of just more charts.
