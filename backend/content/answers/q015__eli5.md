question: "When would you fine-tune an LLM instead of using prompting or RAG?"

Say you've got a talented new hire. You can leave a sticky-note of instructions each morning (that's **prompting**), hand them a reference binder to look things up (that's **RAG**, retrieval), or send them to a training course that permanently changes how they work (that's **fine-tuning**, which actually updates the model's internal settings). Reach for fine-tuning when you need a consistent style, tone, or format that's awkward to spell out every time, when you're teaching a specialized behavior, or when you want shorter prompts and faster, cheaper responses because the skill is baked in, and you have plenty of example data. But if the real need is *facts that change*, like policies or documents, prefer RAG, because fine-tuning bakes knowledge in and it quickly goes stale. Prompting is best for quick experiments. Often teams combine a little fine-tuning with RAG.
