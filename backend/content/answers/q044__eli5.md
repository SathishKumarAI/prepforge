question: "How do you handle an imbalanced classification dataset?"

Suppose 99 of every 100 transactions are legit and only 1 is fraud. A lazy model can score 99% accuracy by simply labeling *everything* legit — like a security guard who waves every single person through and is technically right almost always, yet catches zero thieves. That's why accuracy lies on imbalanced data.

To fix it, you rebalance or reweight. **Oversample** the rare class (a method called SMOTE even invents realistic new examples of it), **undersample** the common class, or tell the model that mistakes on the rare class **cost more** (class weights). Just as important, judge the model with **precision, recall, and F1** — measures focused on how well it catches the rare cases — not plain accuracy.

And always do any resampling only inside your training data, never the test set, or you'll fool yourself with leaked information and cheerful, meaningless scores.
